Our Story

Her Perspective

I first met Ankur when I started working at Karma WiFi in 2015. I thought he was some crazy party animal who was too cool to talk to me, and we butted heads about everything. After a few months we started becoming friends and once we started talking, we couldn’t stop. We quickly became close friends, and then more (to the amusement of our coworkers).

About a month into dating, I found a cheap flight to Iceland six months in the future and impulsively asked Ankur if he’d go with me on a 10 day trip. He said yes, and that was the beginning of an adventurous life together.

I always thought that it was corny nonsense when people said that when you found the right person for you, you would just know. But then I met Ankur, and everything I thought I knew got turned completely upside down.

Right before our Iceland trip in 2016, I got a job offer from a company in California and we decided that despite all the odds stacked against us (we did a SWOT analysis), we would move to Los Angeles together with the two cats, Lardy and MauMau.

Those who know me know that I am not usually a risk-taker. That’s what I love most about Ankur -- he brings out a childlike spirit in everyone he meets and makes it easy to dream and then make those dreams come true. Nothing is impossible for him, and he breaks all the rules...including:

1. Never say ‘I love you’ on the first date.
2. Don’t commit to a trip together far in the future if you just started dating someone.
3. Don’t move in with someone < 6 months into dating.
4. Definitely don’t move across the country with someone < 6 months into dating.

Events

September 19th

Sangeet and Garba
5PM - 10PM

Among the many pre-wedding events that occur prior to South Asian wedding ceremonies, the Sangeet is where the party begins. Friends and family perform songs and choreographed dances for the bride and groom as part of the wedding celebrations. It'll be a fun and crazy night full of dancing!

Garba is a form of circular dance that originated in Gujrat (where Ankur's family is from). People dance around the center in a circle making sweeping motions with their arms. September 20th

Haldi
10AM-12PM

For the haldi ceremony, everyone gets to "paint" the bride and groom with a turmeric paste. Because turmeric can stain bright yellow, wear an old pair of khakis/jeans and a shirt that can live with a stain.

Mendhi
7PM - 12AM

Mehndi, otherwise known as henna, is a paste associated with positive spirits and good luck. Indian wedding tradition calls for a Mehndi ceremony to be held the night before the wedding as a way of wishing the bride good health and prosperity as she makes her journey on to marriage. This will be a night of celebrating, dancing, singing, and partying.

September 21st

Wedding Ceremony and Reception
10AM - 2AM

This is the main event in the entire wedding when the bride and the groom officially marry. It will include a number of traditional Indian and Chinese wedding ceremonies.

September 22nd

Brunch
10AM - 12PM

Time to recover from the weekend with a delicious post-wedding brunch.

  Dress Code

What do I wear?

Sangeet and Garba 5PM - 10PM

Ethnic Indian wear
Wear something with bright colors that you can dance in. Try wearing a lehenga, a long skirt with a crop top, a colorful suit, pyjamas, bright kurtas with pants.

Haldi 10PM - 12PM

Dressy Casual attire
Since Haldi is a bright yellow paste, wear something you don't mind getting messy. Think blouse and jeans, salwar kameez, kurta with pants.

Mehndi 7PM - 12AM

Ethnic Indian wear
Ladies can wear something bright & vivid and gentlemen can be on their vibrant kurtas with pants or pyjamas along with an optional shawl.

Wedding Ceremony6PM-12AM

Indo-western
This is your time to shine! Ladies can flaunt their unconventional sarees, lehengas, anarkalis, dresses and the list goes on whereas gentlemen can wear any kind of suit or just blazer. If you don't like to wear a suit/blazer, you can do a high-neck (band gala) waistcoat & shirt combo.

Reception 1PM - 5PM

Ethnic indian wear
Ladies can wear something bright & vivid and gentlemen can be on their vibrant kurtas with pants or pyjamas along with an optional shawl.
