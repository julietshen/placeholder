Events

August 6th

Sangeet and Garba
5PM - 10PM

Among the many pre-wedding events that occur prior to South Asian wedding ceremonies, the Sangeet is where the party begins. Friends and family perform songs and choreographed dances for the bride and groom as part of the wedding celebrations. It'll be a fun and crazy night full of dancing!

Garba is a form of circular dance that originated in Gujrat (where Ankur's family is from). People dance around the center in a circle making sweeping motions with their arms. This routine is followed by Dandhiya Raas, which is a structured dance with wooden sticks.

August 7th

Haldi
10AM-12PM

For the haldi ceremony, everyone gets to "paint" the bride and groom with a turmeric paste. Because turmeric can stain bright yellow, wear an old pair of khakis/jeans and a shirt that can live with a stain.

Mendhi
7PM - 12AM

Mehndi, otherwise known as henna, is a paste associated with positive spirits and good luck. Indian wedding tradition calls for a Mehndi ceremony to be held the night before the wedding as a way of wishing the bride good health and prosperity as she makes her journey on to marriage.
